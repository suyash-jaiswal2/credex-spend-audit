# ECONOMICS.md

## What a converted lead is worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI credits. Assume:
- Average deal: $500/mo in credits (conservative for a 10–50 person startup)
- Gross margin on credits: ~20% (buying at discount, selling below retail)
- Monthly gross profit per customer: $100
- Average customer lifetime: 12 months
- **LTV per converted customer: ~$1,200**

High-savings segment (>$500/mo identified): likely larger teams, higher spend.
Conservative LTV for this segment: $2,000–$5,000.

Blended CAC at launch: effectively $0 (all organic). If Credex runs paid ads
later at $2 CPC → $200 per sale at 1% end-to-end conversion.

## Conversion funnel

- Audit completed → email captured: target **25%**
  (value is shown first, email asked after — this should be achievable)
- Email captured → consultation booked: target **10%**
  (only high-savings cases are prompted; these users have clear ROI)
- Consultation booked → credit purchase: target **30%**
  (Credex's own sales process; not in scope for this tool)

**End-to-end: 100 audits → 25 leads → 2.5 consultations → 0.75 sales**
Average LTV $1,200 → **$900 revenue per 100 audits**

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 months

$1M ARR = ~$83k MRR = ~70 new customers/month at $1,200 LTV
(or ~35/month at $2,400 LTV for the high-savings segment)

To get 70 customers/month:
- Need ~9,300 audits/month (at 0.75% end-to-end conversion)
- At 25% email capture: 2,325 leads/month
- This requires ~300 audit completions/day

**What has to be true:**
1. The tool goes semi-viral — at least 2–3 HN/Reddit moments in 18 months
2. Credex builds an SEO content strategy around "AI tool cost" keywords
3. The embeddable widget gets picked up by 5–10 newsletters or blogs
4. Credex's existing sales team converts consultations at ≥30%

Rough but achievable. The key leverage point is the widget — if bloggers
embed it, every reader is a qualified lead with zero ongoing CAC.

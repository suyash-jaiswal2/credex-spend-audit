# GTM.md

## Exact target user
Not "startups" — specifically: **engineering managers and CTOs at seed-to-Series A
startups (5–50 people)** who personally approve the company credit card for SaaS tools.
They're technical enough to understand the audit output but busy enough that they've
never sat down to benchmark their AI spend.

Secondary: **indie hackers and solo founders** running 2–5 AI tools on personal cards,
who feel vague guilt about the monthly bill but don't know what normal looks like.

## What they Google/scroll before wanting this
- "cursor vs github copilot 2026"
- "is claude team worth it for small startup"
- "reduce openai api costs"
- Scrolling a tweet like "we cut our AI bill by 60% by switching from X to Y"
- Reading a Hacker News thread: "Ask HN: how much are you spending on AI tools?"

## Where they hang out
- **r/SaaS** and **r/MachineLearning** — cost threads get traction
- **Hacker News** — Show HN posts with real numbers get front page
- **Indie Hackers** — the community actively shares tool stacks and costs
- **Linear/Notion/Vercel Discord servers** — eng managers in their natural habitat
- **X/Twitter** — following @levelsio, @pieterlevels, @marc_louvion type accounts
- Slack groups: Online Geniuses, Ramen Club, Midwest Dev Chat

## First 100 users in 30 days, $0 budget

1. **Show HN post** — "Show HN: I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend"
   with real numbers from a beta tester. Target Tuesday 9am US Eastern.
   Realistic outcome: 200–800 visitors, 40–120 completions.

2. **Post in r/SaaS and r/Entrepreneur** — frame as "I was shocked how much we
   were overpaying — built this to check" with a screenshot of a real audit result.

3. **Cold DM 20 founders on X** — find people who've tweeted about AI tool costs
   in the last 30 days. Personalise each DM with their specific tool stack.

4. **Indie Hackers milestone post** — "I built and shipped this in 7 days" with the
   DEVLOG as the story. This community loves transparent build logs.

5. **Comment in relevant HN threads** — whenever someone asks about AI costs,
   drop a useful comment and mention the tool naturally.

## Unfair distribution channel
Credex already has relationships with AI infrastructure buyers. A direct email to
their existing customer list — "we built a free audit tool, here's what your peers
are overpaying" — converts at 10–20× cold traffic because the trust is already there.
No other individual candidate has this channel.

## Week-1 traction if it works
- 150+ audit completions
- 40+ email captures (25%+ conversion from completion to email)
- 1 HN front page appearance or viral tweet (>500 engagements)
- 3–5 inbound Credex consultation requests from high-savings audits
